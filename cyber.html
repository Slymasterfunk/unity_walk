Cyber Racism

What is Cyber Racism?

Any use of information and communication technologies to transmit racist attitudes and behavior, including the transfer of racially offensive content intended to cause harm or distress to another person.



The nature of rapidly developing technologies means that areas that were once considered private spaces are now public. The increased use of the internet and the development of social networking sites and maps, for example, mean that comments classified as ‘racist,’ typically between one person and another, can now be sent to thousands of people within seconds. This has more severe consequences for the target of racist behavior.


In addition, the increased use and accessibility of technology means that everyone can self-publish on the internet. This has inevitably led to many factually incorrect websites. It has also led to the development of web content that is racially motivated. The increased use of social media and online gaming has also provided forums for racist material.
